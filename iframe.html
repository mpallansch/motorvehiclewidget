Glossary of Terms

Automated Red-Light Enforcement: red-light cameras that capture an image of a vehicle that runs a red light to detect and prevent red-light runners.

Automated Speed-Camera Enforcement: speed cameras that capture an image of a vehicle that is speeding to detect and prevent speeders.

Alcohol Interlocks: a device that keeps the vehicle from starting unless the driver has a blood alcohol concentration below a pre-set limit.

Sobriety Checkpoints: predetermined locations at which law enforcement officers stop vehicles to check whether the driver is impaired.

Saturation Patrols: consist of a large number of law enforcement officers patrolling a specific area for a set time to increase visibility of enforcement.

Bicycle Helmet Laws for Children: laws to encourage parents to require their children to use helmets.

Universal Motorcycle Helmet Laws: laws that require motorcycle riders to wear a helmet while riding on public roads. The universal laws apply to all motorcycle operators and passengers.

Primary Enforcement of Seat Belt Laws: police officers can stop a vehicle and write a ticket for anyone not buckled up.

High-Visibility Enforcement for Seat Belts and Child Restraint and Booster Laws: short (typically lasting for two weeks), intense, highly publicized periods of increased belt law enforcement, frequently using checkpoints (in states where they are permitted), saturation patrols, or enforcement zones.

License Plate Impoundment: a sanction that affects a Driving While Intoxicated (DWI) offender's license plate and prevents the offender from driving the vehicle while the sanction is in effect.

Limits on Diversion and Plea Agreements: diversion programs defer sentencing while a DWI offender participates in some form of alcohol education or treatment. A plea agreement allows a DWI offender to negotiate the charges against him or her or the terms of his or her sentence. Limits on diversions and plea agreements restricts the number of times an offender can defer his or her sentence and/or negotiate charges and the terms of his or her sentence.

Vehicle Impoundment: a sanction affecting a DWI offender's vehicle that allows seizure or storage of the vehicle for a designated period of time.

In-Person License Renewal: drivers must appear in person to renew an expiring license, pay the license fee, and have new pictures taken for their licenses.

Increased Fines for Seat Belt Use: higher penalties for seat belt use violations. Low fines may not convince non-users to buckle up and may send a message that seat belt use laws are not taken seriously.
